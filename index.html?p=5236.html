Book of the Month: The Great Expedition

May 30, 2011Literacypicture books, writerSquiggleMum

I’m a big fan of local author/illustrator Peter Carnavas.  Like myself, Peter is a primary school teacher so he knows what makes a great picture book.  With New Frontier behind him, Carnavas has been bringing out book after fabulous book.

His latest offering and fifth picture book is The Great Expedition, an interpretation of the story about Australian explorers Burke and Wills. While the text reflects upon many of the trials faced by the team of early explorers, the illustrations instead depict children on their own neighbourhood adventure.

The Great Expedition is about a team of brave young explorers who cross wild country to deliver a valuable parcel.  The journey is punishing.  Some of the explorers don’t make it.  But in the end, we are left with two courageous souls to admire and remember for many years to come.

(Illustration roughs from Peter’s blog)

Exploring the illustrations and their connection to the text is a delight, and the book is a wonderful introduction to Australian history for younger readers.  The book was only released this month, so may not be in your library as yet.  You can purchase copies of The Great Expedition from good bookstores, or from New Frontier online for $24.95 (hardback).
