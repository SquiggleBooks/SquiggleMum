I Am Not Perfect

December 3, 2009Faith, MumsSquiggleMum

I am not a perfect person.  Just someone who tries hard, loves life, and wears a smile – most of the time.

I am not a perfect Christian.  Just someone who knows she needs God in her life.

I am not a perfect mum.  Just someone who is willing to share her parenting experiences with others.

I am not a perfect wife.  Just someone who loves her husband as much on the bad days as on the good.

I am not a perfect writer.  Just someone who likes words and hates typos.

I am not a perfect housekeeper.  Just someone who believes in keeping things clean enough to be healthy while understanding that fun is messy.

I am not a perfect blogger.  Just someone who is enjoying the learning curve and choosing to stick at it.

I am not a perfect MOPS coordinator.  Just someone who loves mums and coordinates a great team.

I am not perfect.  I’m just me.

I know what you’re thinking.  No one expects me to be perfect.

No one expects you to be perfect either. So why do we put so much pressure on ourselves? This week let’s let go of perfect, and just be the women, mothers, wives, writers, bloggers, leaders… that we were created to be.

Bless you heaps. xx

What Will They Remember? (Parenting Aus)

December 1, 2009MumsSquiggleMum

Today is the day!  Summer is offically here, and now that it’s December Christmas is upon us.  The festive flurry is in full swing in the retail sector, credit cards are getting a work out and Santa is in every department store.  And it’s only the 1st of December.  But is this really what you want your kids to remember about Christmas?  I’m over at Parenting Australia today sharing my thoughts on what I hope my kids will look back on… one day.  Here’s a snippet:

One day my children won’t be children any more. They’ll be grownups. They’ll probably have children of their own. And every Christmas they’ll look back on their childhood. More than any gift I ever buy them, I want them to remember Christmas lullabies. More than the food I prepare, I want them to remember the way we decorated the tree. I so badly want them to remember quiet Christmas Eves more than a cranky, flustered Mummy with a to-do list longer than a stocking. And I don’t mind whether they carry on my traditions, or make their own (someone please remind me about this in 2029) as long as their focus is on their family.
