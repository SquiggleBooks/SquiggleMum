]]> http://www.squigglemum.com/win/tin-can-play-and-milo-giveaway/feed/ 78 Sport vs Play http://www.squigglemum.com/activities/activities-outdoor/sport-vs-play/ http://www.squigglemum.com/activities/activities-outdoor/sport-vs-play/#comments Thu, 27 Oct 2011 07:11:43 +0000 http://squigglemum.com/?p=5645 This week I was invited by Cricket Queensland to meet legendary cricketer Allan Border and hear about the in2CRICKET program for kids. The program aims to involve primary school aged kids in a shorter version of the game (which doesn’t take up a whole Saturday…)

I enjoyed talking to Allan about how outdoor play has changed over the last couple of generations.  Gone are the days of all kids climbing trees.  Gone are the monkey bars from playgrounds.  Gone are the games of cricket in the street with the neighbourhood kids.  And gone are the mums who say, “Off you go and play, but be back by dinner!”

The result is that many of today’s kids have poor gross motor skills and a lack of confidence in their physical abilities.  This is being seen by early childhood educators and sports coaches alike.

Organised sports activities can be a great way to develop your child’s skills, but sport doesn’t take the place of all important free play.  Different things are learned in each, as I discussed on air this week.

(If you missed it, you can catch the podcast here: 25oct_SquiggleMum_podcast)

The benefits of free play include:

  • general gross motor awareness and development
  • problem solving
  • independence
  • creativity and imagination

While the benefits of organised sport include:

  • specific skill development
  • discipline
  • commitment
  • cooperation
  • “belonging” to a team

My opinion?

I think sport is great, but it shouldn’t come at the expense of free play.  Kids need plenty of free play outdoors during their early childhood and early primary school years, so that they can step confidently into organised sports.

What’s your opinion?  Are your kids involved in organised sport?
